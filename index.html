About

Yogacharya B.K.S. Iyengar was born in Bellur, India on December 14, 1918. Growing up as an unheathy child who suffered from many diseases in his early life, Iyengar found yoga as a way to treat his physical ailments. He asana studied in Mysore with his guru, Sri Krishnamacharya, and moved to Pune with his guru's blessings to teach the asana practice. In his 95 years of life, Iyengar taught yoga asana for almost 77 years, authored 14 books, and educated people around the world about what we consider to be modern-day yoga. Up until his death in 2014, Iyengar claimed to have practiced asana for three hours a day and pranayamas for at least an hour.
